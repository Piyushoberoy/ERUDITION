VERB

Averb is a word that tells or asserts something about a person or thing.

Reeta is the name of my neighbour's granddaughter, she loves to play football with me. Whenever Reeta saw a herd of elephants she felt very excited and always plea her grandfather to make a ride of it.
    Types of verb:-
  1. Finite verbs
  2. Infinite verbs

Finite verbs:-

Finite verbs are those verbs which need a subject and can form a sentence with the aid of another verb.

  1. We rob banks.
    2. Here "rob" is finite verb.
  2. I know how to whistle.
    3. Here "know" is finit verb.
This thing will be more clear soon.

Infinite verbs:-

These verbs do not need a subject and can not form a sentence without the aid of another verb. It usually preceded by "to".

  1. My family likes to celebrate birthdays together.
    2. Here "celebrate" is Infinite verb.
  2. My sister went to college to become a doctor.
    3. Here "become" is Infinite verb.

Based on object taken verbs can be diivded into two forms.

  1. Transitive verbs
  2. Intransitive verbs

Transitive verbs:-

These verbs have objects after them.

  1. He plays guitar.
    2. Here "plays" is Transitive verb, since it needed object "guitar" to make a meaningful sentence.
  2. The speaker discussed different marketing strategies in the video.
    3. Here "discussed" is Transitive verb, since it needed object "different marketing strategies" to make a meaningful sentence.

Intransitive verbs:-

These verbs do not have objects after them.

  1. The students arrived at the residency in Houston.
    2. Here "arrived" is Intransitive Verb, since it does not need object "Houston". If object comes just after Intransitive verb "arrived" it will caus error.
  2. The patient's health deteriorated quickly.
    3. Here "deteriorated" is Intransitive verb, otherwise it will cause error in the sentence.

NOTE:- Each verb whether it is Finite or Infinite will be Transitive or Intransitive.
