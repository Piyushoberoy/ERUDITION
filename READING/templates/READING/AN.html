AN

Conjunction is word which joins together sentences and sometimes words

Tanya is best friend of Abhinav and they both are more than a best a friend.
    Types of Conjunction:-
  1. Co-ordinating Conjunctions
  2. Sub-ordinating Conjunctions

Co-ordinating Conjunctions:-

Those words which join two separate sentences.

  1. He is poor but he is charitable.
    2. Here "but" is co-ordinate conjunction as it is combining two separate sentences i.e., "he is poor" and another one "he is charitable".
  2. He came and helped me.
    3. Here "and" is co-ordinate conjunction as it is combining two separate sentences i.e., "he came" and another one "he helped me".

Sub-ordinating Conjunctions:-

A subordinating conjunction is a word or phrase that links a dependent clause to an independent clause.

  1. He came here after I went away.
    2. Here "after" is Sub-ordinate conjunction, since dependent clause "he came here" links with independent clause "I went away".
  2. He failed although he worked hard
    3. Here "although" is Sub-ordinate conjunction.
