NOUN

A Noun is a word used as the name of a person, place, or thing.

Reeta is the name of my neighbour's granddaughter, she loves to play football with me. Whenever Reeta saw a herd of elephants she felt very excited and always plea her grandfather to make a ride of it.
    Types of nouns:-
  1. Common Noun
  2. Proper Noun
  3. Collective Noun
  4. Material Noun
  5. Abstract Noun

Common Noun:-

A common noun is a name given in common to every person or thing of the same class or kind.

  1. The dog is a faithful animal
    2. Here "dog" is common noun as it is common to its class or kind.
  2. There are fourty trees on the road
    3. Here "trees" are common noun as it is common to its class, no matter whether there are different trees or not.

Proper Noun:-

A proper noun is the name of some particular person or place-as; Ram, Ramayan, The Taj.

  1. Kalidas is the Shakespeare of India
    2. Here "Shakespeare" is proper noun as it is the name of famous poet.
  2. He was the Rustum of his times
    3. Here "Rustum" is proper noun as it is again the name of legendary hero of Persion mythological stories.

A Collective Noun:-

A collective noun is the name of a number of persons things together and spoken of as one whole-as; class, army, nation, fleet, family, herd.

  1. A heap of rubbish
    2. Here "heap" is collective noun as "heap" refers to large number.
  2. A pack of cards.
    3. Here "pack" is again a countable part so it is collective noun.

A Material Noun:-

A material noun is concerned with metals and things-as; Gold, silver, stone, cotton, wool.

  1. The thief was led in irons.
    2. Here "iron" is a material, so it is a material noun.
  2. He deals in precious stones.
    3. Here "stone" is a material, so it is material noun.

An Abstract Noun:-

A abstract noun is usually the name of a quality, action, or state considered apart from the oject to which it belongs; as:-

  • Quality:- Goodness, whiteness, honesty, wisdom
  • Action:- Laughter, movement, hatred
  • State:- Childhood, boyhood, youth

    1. He is a justice of the place.
      2. When an Abstract Noun is preceded by an article it becomes Common Noun. That's why "justice" is considered as Abstract Noun.
    2. Kashmir is famous for her natural beauties.
      3. When an Abstract Noun is used in the plural, becomes Common Noun. So, here "beauties" are Abstract Noun.
