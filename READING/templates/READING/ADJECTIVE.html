ADJECTIVE

Adjective qualifies a Noun or Pronoun. A word used with noun to describe, or point out, the person, animal, place or thing : or to tell the number or quantity is called an Adjective

Ram is a person with innate goodness. He is always an adherent of truth and dharma.
    Types of Adjective:-
  1. Descriptive Adjective
  2. Numeral Adjective
  3. Quantitative Adjective
  4. Demonstrative Adjective
  5. Interrogative Adjective
  6. Possesive Adjective
  7. Proper Adjective
  8. Exclamatory Adjective

Descriptive Adjective:-

Adjectives of Quality or Descriptive Adjectives show the kind of quality of a person or thing. Ex. Beautiful, large, small etc.

  1. Miss. Sanyogita is very beautiful.
    2. Here "beautiful" is describing the quality of "Mis. Sanyogita".
  2. The foolish old crow tries to sing.
    3. Here "foolish" is descibing the quality of crow.

Numeral Adjective:-

Adjectives of number or Numeral Adjectives show how many persons or things are meant. This adjective represents numeral value. Ex. Eight, few, second, some etc.

  1. Mr. Banerji's fourtyfive years of dedication lead hime to discovery of an advanced Homeopathy.
    2. Here "fourtyfive" is a definite numerical value that is showing the work duration of "Mr. Banerji".
  2. Most of the boys are selected for hockey.
    3. Here "most" is an indefinite numeral value that is delineating the numer of boys selected for game "hockey".

Quantitative Adjective:-

Adjectives of quantity show how much quantity of a thing is meant. Ex. Some, little, whole, one fourth etc.

  1. One by two of India's Kashmir is occupied by Pakistan and China.
    2. Here "one by two" is quantity of "India's Kashmir that has been occupied by Pakistan and China".
  2. Many precious metals of India had been occupied by Britishers and till now England had not returned it back to nation.
    3. Here "many" is quantity of "precious metals of India".

Demonstrative Adjective:-

Demonstrative Adjectives point out which person or thing is meant, ex. This, that, those, such etc.

  1. This girl is taller than Rosie.
    2. Here "this" is demonstrating to only "Rosie".
  2. Give me that blue water bottle.
    3. Here "give" is pointing to only that "water" which is inside the bottle.

Interrogative Adjective:-

What, which, and whose, when they are used with nouns to ask questions, are called Interrogative Adjectives.

  1. Whose book is this?
    2. Here "whose" is Interrogative adjective that is used with noun "book".
  2. Which book on proofreading do you recommend?
    3. Here "which" is Interrogative adjective that is used with noun "book".

Possesive Adjective:-

We use Possessive adjectives to express who owns or possesses something. This is used in front of a noun. Ex. Own, my, our etc.

  1. He is hisown master.
    2. Here "own" is Possesive adjective that is expressing his quality.
  2. Your child is not doing well in the school.
    3. Here "your" is Possesive adjective that is possesses to child.

Proper Adjective:-

Adjectives formed from proper nouns are called Proper Adjectives. Ex. Indian, British, Chinese etc.

  1. The Indian professor is teaching in the class.
    2. Here "Indian" is a Proper adjectives.
  2. Jumbo was a famousAfrican elephant.
    3. Here "African" is a Proper adjective.

Exclamatory Adjective:-

The word what is sometimes used as an Exclamatory Adjective.

  1. What a genius!
  2. What a blessing!
