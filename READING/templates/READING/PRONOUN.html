PRONOUN

A word that is used instead of a noun is called Pronoun.

Lord Rama is one of the most widely worshipped Hindu deities, the embodiment of chivalry and virtue. His story is told in the epic poems the Mahabharata and the Ramayana.
    Types of pronouns:-
  1. Relative Pronoun
  2. Possessive Pronoun
  3. Reflexive Pronoun
  4. Demonstrative Pronoun
  5. Interrogative Pronoun
  6. Personal Pronoun
  7. Reciprocal Pronoun

Relative Pronoun:-

Pronouns that are used to relate one part of the sentence to another.

  1. The community hall, in which the function was held, had maximum space of about 200 people.
    2. Here "which" is relative pronoun as it is relating one part of sentence i.e., community hall with another part i.e., function, which is going to be held in community hall.
  2. This is the place where we met.
    3. Here "where" is relative pronoun as meeting is related to that particular place.

Some Relative Pronoun:-

  1. That
  2. Who
  3. Whoever
  4. Which
  5. Whichever
  6. Whom
  7. Whomever

Possessive Pronoun:-

Those pronouns that are used to show possession.

  1. The cat is mine
    2. The possessive pronoun "mine" replaces the phrase "my cat" which would make the sentence repetitive.
  2. I believe this is yours
    3. The possessive pronoun "yours" implies second-person ownership of "this.""

Some Possessive Pronoun:-

  1. Mine
  2. Yours
  3. his
  4. hers
  5. theirs
  6. its
  1. Avoid apostrophes.
  2. Try not to confuse them with possessive adjectives.

Reflexive Pronoun:-

It is a type of pronoun used when the subject of a sentence is the same as the object of the sentence.

  1. Dad and I painted the trailer ourselves
  2. Please make yourselves at home while you wait.

List of all Reflexive Pronouns:-

  1. Myself
  2. Yourself
  3. Herself
  4. Himself
  5. Oneself
  6. Itself
  7. Ourselves
  8. Themselves
  9. Yourselves

Demonstrative Pronoun:-

Those pronouns that are used to point to specific objects.

  1. Are those your shoes?
    2. Here "shoes" are that particular pointing object. That's why "those" is used here.
  2. Because of their bad behavior, none of the children were given allowances.
    3. Only there is a specific child whose behaiour is talking about. That's why "there" is used.

Some Demonstrative Pronouns:-

  1. This
  2. That
  3. These
  4. Their
  5. There
  6. Those

Interrogative Pronoun:-

These are those pronouns that are used to ask questions.

  1. Which of these ladies is your mother?
    2. Here "shoes" are that particular pointing object. That's why "those" is used here.
  2. What do you want for dinner?

Some Interrogative Pronouns:-

  1. Who
  2. What
  3. When
  4. Why
  5. Where

Personal Pronoun:-

These are simple pronouns that are used to substitute proper names.

  1. He is my brother.
  2. We are going to Yamunanagar.

Some Personal Pronouns:-

  1. I
  2. You
  3. He
  4. She
  5. We
  6. They
  7. Him
  8. Her
  9. His
  10. It

Reciprocal Pronoun:-

Those pronouns that are pronouns that are used to express a mutual relationship.

  1. Suru and Piyu enjoying party with each other.
  2. Students of IIT helpsone another.

Some Reciprocal Pronouns:-

  1. Each other
  2. One another
