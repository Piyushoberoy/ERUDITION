ADVERB

The adverb is a word which modifies the meaning of a verb, an adjective, or another Adverb; as, quickly, late, soon, before, always.

Reeta is the name of my neighbour's granddaughter, she loves to play football with me. Whenever Reeta saw a herd of elephants she felt very excited and always plea her grandfather to make a ride of it.
    Types of adverb:-
  1. Simple Adverbs
  2. Interrogative Adverbs
  3. Introductory Adverbs
  4. Relative Adverbs
  5. Demonstrative Adverbs

Simple Adverbs:-

Simple Adverbs contain only one word and they are the most used Adverbs.

  1. I am visiting my grandmother this weekend.
    2. Here, "this" is pointing towards that particular weekend.
  2. The flowers on her balcony are extremely beautiful.

Interrogative Adverbs:-

It is an adverb that asks a question about place, time, manner, or reason.

  1. Where do you live?
  2. When are you moving

Introductory Adverbs:-

Adverbs that come at the beginning of a sentence are called introductory adverbs.

  1. Tomorrow I shall go there.
  2. Always you are welcome.

Relative Adverbs:-

The relative adverbs are "where," "when," and "why." A relative adverb is an adverb that heads an adjective clause.

  1. I know the town where you live.
    2. Here "where" introduces an adjective clause describing a place.
  2. They're talking about an era when a promise was a promise.
    3. Here "when" introduces an adjective clause describing a time.

Demonstrative Adverbs:-

It express various information including time, location, manner or degree.

  1. Give me that blue water bottle.
    2. Here, "that" demonstrates that he/her needs blue water bottle.
  2. This time I won't fail you.
    3. Here, "this" demonstrates that the person would not fail at him.
